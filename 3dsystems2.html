Overview

My role as a Research Intern at 3D Systems provided an incredible opportunity to work within one of the leading companies in the 3D printing industry. Tasked with developing a process for creating customized, 3D-printed casts for fracture patients, I immersed myself in an innovative project that combined medical applications with cutting-edge technology. This experience gave me an in-depth look at research and development and made personalized medical devices seem like more of a reality than ever before.

During my first week, I focused on understanding the requirements of medical-grade 3D printing and the complex nature of personalized healthcare products. I reviewed existing research on 3D-printed medical devices and learned about 3D scanning and printing techniques tailored to medical applications. This foundational knowledge equipped me with both a technical and a user-centered perspective, enabling me to align my solution with the specific needs of fracture patients.

The primary goal of my project was to establish a scan-to-print process that would allow for the customization of casts, improving patient comfort and reducing the limitations associated with traditional casts. I began by conducting cost analyses to ensure that the process would be feasible for clinical use, analyzing materials and production expenses. Working iteratively, I explored design options for the casts, balancing structural integrity, weight, and comfort. I also conducted tests on the scan-to-print workflow to prototype these casts as a proof-of-concept, refining each iteration based on feedback. This hands-on experience taught me how to integrate technical skills with practical considerations, such as cost and user needs.

Reflecting on my internship at 3D Systems, I realize how impactful this experience was in expanding my understanding of the R&D process within a major industry player. This project not only sharpened my technical and analytical skills but also deepened my appreciation for personalized healthcare. By prototyping a product with real-world applications, I saw firsthand how innovation can directly impact people’s lives.


Key Skills

Research & Development (R&D): Researched and developed a customized 3D-printed cast solution for fracture patients using advanced 3D scanning and printing techniques, contributing to innovative healthcare applications.
Report Writing: Created comprehensive reports detailing cost analysis, design iterations, and test results, effectively communicating findings and recommendations to support product development decisions.
Prototyping: Executed iterative design and testing processes for cast prototypes, refining functionality, patient comfort, and production efficiency through hands-on testing and feedback.
Industry Analytics: Conducted cost and material analysis to assess feasibility, balancing quality and affordability for potential clinical applications, and aligning project outcomes with industry standards in medical 3D printing.
