My Kumon Journey

My journey with Kumon began not as an employee, but as a young student eager to get ahead in school. Enrolled at an early age, I dove headfirst into Kumon's unique learning methods, which promoted independence and understanding of fundamentals. This experience not only benefited me academically, but also instilled a lifelong appreciation for effective education—a passion that eventually led me back to Kumon as a team member.

Years later, the opportunity arose to join the Kumon team. Remembering the impact the program had on my own development, I was excited to contribute to others' learning journeys. Becoming an employee felt like a natural progression—a chance to give back to the community that had significantly shaped my early education.

Working at Kumon was enlightening. The after-school hours had the center in constant chaos. Students of all ages poured in, each with unique needs and learning styles. Collaborating with fellow team members was essential to manage the fast-paced environment effectively. We coordinated to ensure that each student received the attention they required. Communicating with children demanded patience and clarity, while interactions with parents required professionalism and empathy. These experiences honed my ability to connect with people from diverse backgrounds and age groups.

One of the significant challenges I faced was adapting instructional techniques to suit individuals. Some students understand concepts quickly, while others need more time and different approaches. This required me to be flexible and creative in my explanations. Additionally, balancing the immediate demands of assisting students with the administrative tasks of the center heightened my multitasking abilities. Overcoming these challenges honed my problem-solving skills and taught me the value of patience and persistence.

The onset of the COVID-19 pandemic brought unforeseen changes. As safety concerns grew, the difficult decision was made to suspend in-person sessions, which eventually led me to leave my position at Kumon. The abrupt end was disheartening, but it showed me the importance of adaptability in the face of unexpected circumstances. It was a stark reminder that even well-laid plans could change.


My 1.5 years at Kumon were a period of immense personal growth. I developed strong communication skills and learned to convey complex ideas in accessible ways. The teamwork in a high-energy environment taught me collaboration and leadership. These skills have proven invaluable in my academics and extracurricular activities alike, providing a solid foundation for future challenges.
